Create a deterministic incident runbook from the three source files in the
current directory: incident.json, observations.csv, and service-context.md.

Use Read to inspect every source. Use Write to create runbook.md. Do not create,
edit, or delete any other file.

The runbook must contain these headings in order:

1. `# Incident runbook: <incident id>`
2. `## Situation`
3. `## Evidence`
4. `## Immediate response`
5. `## Recovery checks`
6. `## Communications`

Under Situation, emit bullets for Severity, Service, Region, Started, and
Customer impact in that order. Under Evidence, emit one bullet per observation
in CSV order using `<signal> is <value> (threshold: <threshold>).` Under
Immediate response, emit the four approved actions in service-context order as
a numbered Markdown list (`1.` through `4.`).
Format them as `Page <team> through PagerDuty service \`<route>\`.`, `Freeze
<service> deployments.`, `Compare payments-gateway timeouts between <region>
and us-east.`, and `If payments-gateway timeouts remain above <threshold> for 10
minutes, shift payment traffic from <region> to us-east.` Under Recovery checks,
use `Confirm <signal> is below <threshold> for 15 continuous minutes.` for each
observation in CSV order. Under Communications, use `Post an update in <channel>
every 15 minutes.` Preserve identifiers, values, punctuation, and capitalization
from the sources. Do not invent diagnosis or remediation.

Read runbook.md after writing it and confirm all facts are source-backed. Finish
with a short statement that runbook.md is complete.
